RECEPTION

The film went on to break the Japanese box office record, beating the previous holder, "Titanic."" It was the most successful film that Studio Ghibli ever produced. It became the first film ever to gross over 200 million dollars before a US release.

Disney agreed to produce an English release at the request of Pixar's John Lasseter. But in response to Studio Ghibli retaining merchandising rights, Disney put relatively little into marketting the film.

After a great critical response, including 4 stars from critic Roger Ebert, and winning the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature, the movie saw more box office success, eventually getting 10 million in the US and 365 million worldwide to date.
